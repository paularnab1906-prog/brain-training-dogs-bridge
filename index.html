Why Your Dog "Doesn't Listen" (It's Not What You Think)

Dogs were bred for work. Herding, hunting, guarding, retrieving — every breed was designed to do something for hours every day. Modern life doesn't give them that outlet. So they create their own "jobs": barking at nothing, chewing shoes, digging holes, pulling on the leash.

These aren't behavior problems. They're under-stimulation problems.

The Science Is Clear

Studies from the University of Bristol's Anthrozoology Institute found that dogs given mental stimulation tasks showed a significant reduction in stress-related behaviors — including excessive barking, destructive chewing, and anxiety — compared to dogs given only physical exercise. A tired body doesn't calm a restless mind.
